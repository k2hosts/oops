Privacy Policy

We treat AI infrastructure as a pipe, not a bucket. We transfer your data, process it, and then forget it ever existed. This document explains exactly what that means in practice.

01

Data Retention

We practice Zero Retention for all inference data. Prompts, responses, and uploaded documents are processed in-memory and discarded when the request closes. No conversation content is ever written to a database or log file on our infrastructure.

02

Privacy Scrubber

A redaction engine runs inside the Cloudflare Worker. If the API crashes, sensitive fields — message content and access keys — are replaced with [REDACTED_PRIVACY] before any error log is written. Your conversation never appears in our error reporting infrastructure.

03

Zero-Retention Identity Layer

Identity and inference are architecturally separated. Cloudflare KV tracks your Discord username, user ID, and token balance for rate-limiting. That store has zero connection to the inference path — who you are is never joined to what you said.

04

Logs & Analytics

We maintain minimal technical metadata to keep the service healthy and to enforce fair-use limits. This is what we store:

  • Metadata Timestamps, token usage counts, and IP addresses, retained for rate-limiting purposes only.
  • Error logs Error codes and stack traces may be logged to diagnose bugs. All user content is scrubbed from these logs by the Privacy Scrubber before they're written.
  • Discord identity Your Discord Username and User ID are logged when you request an access key, solely to issue and manage that key.
05

Model Training

We do not use your data to train, fine-tune, or evaluate any model — ours or anyone else's. Your inputs are yours.

06

Infrastructure & Third Parties

The API runs on Cloudflare Workers. Inference requests may be routed through upstream model providers for execution. Where configurable, we ensure upstream providers are set to non-retention mode for training purposes.

We do not sell, share, or monetise user data in any form.

07

User Responsibility

Users are solely responsible for the inputs provided and the resulting outputs. oopsAPI provides raw model access and does not perform post-processing or content filtering.
